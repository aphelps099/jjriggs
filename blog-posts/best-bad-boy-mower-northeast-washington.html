What makes a good mower for Northeast Washington?

A mower that works in Texas does not always work here. Our properties have features the showroom photos never show:

  • Slopes and uneven ground. Roads, driveways, and pasture edges in NE Washington are rarely flat. A heavy frame, low center of gravity, and real suspension matter on Stevens County hillsides.
  • Long grass and tall weeds. Most acreage owners don't mow weekly. You need a deck that lifts grass before it cuts — not a residential deck that bogs down the second growth gets past 6 inches.
  • Rocks, sticks, and debris. Every property up here hides surprises. A 7-gauge steel deck and reinforced spindles are not optional.
  • Cut quality on the first pass. When you have 5+ acres to mow, you don't have time to go over the same lawn twice. Deck airflow and blade tip speed matter.
  • Service close to home. When a belt blows or a spindle goes, you need a dealer who has the part — not a 3-week shipping wait.

Bad Boy zero-turn vs Bad Boy compact tractor — which do you need?

Most property owners up here end up buying both eventually. Here is how to decide which one comes first.

Zero-turn mower

Best for properties where the main job is keeping grass cut. If you have lawn, pasture clearings, or open ground that needs to be mowed weekly or biweekly during the season, a Bad Boy zero-turn will outwork a tractor with a finishing mower every time. Faster ground speed, tighter turns, better cut quality.

Compact tractor

Best when mowing is one of many jobs — and not the biggest one. If you are also moving snow, hauling firewood, dragging arenas, grading driveways, lifting pallets, or running implements year-round, a Bad Boy compact tractor with a brush hog or finish mower handles all of it.

The realistic answer for most NE Washington properties

Zero-turn for the lawn and the easy pasture. Compact tractor for everything else. Buy the zero-turn first if mowing is your immediate pain point.

How much horsepower and deck size do you need?

This is the single most common question we get. Here is the rough math:

  • Under 1 acre, flat ground: 42-inch deck, 22–24 HP. The Bad Boy ZT Avenger or Maverick 42 is plenty.
  • 1–3 acres of lawn: 48-inch deck, 22–25 HP. The ZT Elite 48 is a bestseller for this size property.
  • 3–7 acres, mixed lawn and rough: 54-inch deck, 24–27 HP. Step up to a Maverick 54 or ZT Elite 54.
  • 7–15 acres, working properties: 60-inch deck, 27–37 HP commercial engine. Maverick HD 60 or Rogue 61 territory.
  • 15+ acres or commercial use: 72-inch deck, 37+ HP, diesel optional. Rogue 72 or Renegade Diesel 61.

A bigger deck cuts more grass per pass, but it also requires more power and more open space to maneuver. If your property has narrow gates, tight tree clusters, or sheds to mow around, do not oversize the deck — you will regret it the first time you can't fit through a 60-inch opening with a 60-inch deck.

Frequently asked questions

Is Bad Boy as good as John Deere or Kubota?
For the price point, yes. Bad Boy uses the same engines (Kawasaki, Kohler, Yanmar), the same transmissions (Hydro-Gear), and the same implement standards (Cat I, standard PTO) as the major brands. The frames are heavier on most Bad Boy models than the residential equivalents from green or orange. Where Bad Boy wins is price-to-spec ratio and dealer relationships. Where the major brands win is resale value and parts ubiquity in markets where Bad Boy doesn't have a dealer.
What is the best Bad Boy mower for 5 acres in NE Washington?
The Bad Boy ZT Elite 54 or Maverick HD 54 with a 24–26 HP Kawasaki engine. The ZT Elite is the budget pick. The Maverick HD is the more durable pick. Both will handle 5 acres of mixed lawn and pasture without complaint.
Does Bad Boy make a diesel mower?
Yes — the Renegade Diesel runs a Kubota three-cylinder diesel and is built for commercial-scale mowing. For most NE Washington property owners under 20 acres, a gas Rogue or Maverick HD is the better fit. Diesel makes sense when you are running 4+ hours a day, 5+ days a week.
Can I run a snow plow or blower on a Bad Boy zero-turn?
Not really. Zero-turns are not designed for snow work — the weight distribution and tire setup are wrong, and most Bad Boy zero-turns don't have a front PTO or a three-point hitch. For snow, use a Bad Boy compact tractor (1025H, 4025, or 4035) with a front blade, rear blower, or front-mount blower. See Best Tractor for Snow Removal in Northeast Washington for the full setup guide.
How long do Bad Boy mowers last?
A residential ZT Elite or Maverick will run 1,500–2,000 hours with normal homeowner maintenance. The commercial-grade Rogue and Renegade lineups will run 3,000+ hours with proper service intervals. The deck and frame outlast the engine on every Bad Boy we have sold.
