What makes a good mower for Northeast Washington?

A mower built for flat Texas suburbs is not the same machine you need up here. Our properties have features the showroom photos never show:

  • Slopes and uneven ground. Roads, driveways, and pasture edges in NE Washington are rarely flat. A heavy frame, low center of gravity, and real suspension matter on Stevens County hillsides.
  • Long grass and tall weeds. Most acreage owners do not mow weekly. You need a deck that lifts grass before it cuts — not a residential deck that bogs down the second growth gets past 6 inches.
  • Rocks, sticks, and debris. Every property up here hides surprises. A 7-gauge steel deck and reinforced spindles are not optional.
  • Cut quality on the first pass. When you have 5+ acres to mow, you do not have time to go over the same ground twice. Deck airflow and blade tip speed matter.
  • Service close to home. When a belt blows or a spindle goes, you need a dealer who has the part — not a 3-week shipping wait.

Bad Boy zero-turn vs Bad Boy compact tractor — which do you need?

Most property owners up here end up buying both eventually. Here is how to decide which one comes first.

Zero-turn mower

Best for properties where the main job is keeping grass cut. If you have lawn, pasture clearings, or open ground that needs to be mowed weekly or biweekly during the season, a Bad Boy zero-turn will outwork a tractor with a finishing mower every time. Faster ground speed, tighter turns, better cut quality.

Compact tractor

Best when mowing is one of many jobs — and not the biggest one. If you are also moving snow, hauling firewood, dragging arenas, grading driveways, lifting pallets, or running implements year-round, a Bad Boy compact tractor with a brush hog or finish mower handles all of it.

The realistic answer for most NE Washington properties

Zero-turn for the lawn and the easy pasture. Compact tractor for everything else. Buy the zero-turn first if mowing is your immediate pain point. Add the tractor when snow, brush, or loader work starts taking too much time.

How much horsepower and deck size do you need?

This is the single most common question we get. Here is the rough math:

  • Under 1 acre, flat ground: 42-inch deck, 22–24 HP. Plenty for a typical residential lot.
  • 1–3 acres of lawn: 48-inch deck, 22–25 HP. The Bad Boy ZT Elite 48 is a bestseller for this size property.
  • 3–7 acres, mixed lawn and rough: 54-inch deck, 24–27 HP. Step up to a Maverick HD 54 or ZT Elite 54.
  • 7–15 acres, working properties: 60-inch deck, 27–37 HP commercial engine. Maverick HD 60 or Rogue 61 territory.
  • 15+ acres or commercial use: 72-inch deck, 37+ HP, diesel optional. Rogue 72 or Renegade Diesel 61.

A bigger deck cuts more grass per pass, but it also requires more power and more open space to maneuver. If your property has narrow gates, tight tree clusters, or sheds to mow around, do not oversize the deck. You will regret it the first time you can't fit through a 60-inch gate with a 60-inch deck.
