Geofencing Facebook Ads

A location-based advertising strategy to reach farmers, ranchers, and property owners in Stevens County and surrounding areas.

01 — Overview

What is Geofencing?

Geofencing uses GPS coordinates to create virtual boundaries around physical locations. When someone enters that boundary with their phone, they become eligible to see your ads—during their visit and for up to 30 days after.

Why it works for JJ Riggs: Your customers are at feed stores, farm supply shops, ag events, and competitor lots. Geofencing lets you reach them exactly when they're thinking about equipment.

02 — Target Locations

Where to Set Fences

Focus on locations where your ideal customers spend time. Start with these high-value targets:

Tier 1: Direct Competitors

Other Tractor Dealerships
Radius: 0.5 miles
Kubota, John Deere, Case IH dealers within 60 miles. Catch shoppers comparing options.

Tier 2: Agriculture Retail

Farm Supply Stores
Radius: 0.25 miles
Coastal Farm & Ranch, North 40 Outfitters, local feed stores. Regular customers with acreage.
Hardware & Building Supply
Radius: 0.25 miles
Home Depot, Lowe's, local lumber yards. Property owners doing projects.

Tier 3: Events & Gathering Spots

County Fairgrounds
Radius: 0.5 miles
Stevens County Fairgrounds during rodeos, 4-H events, livestock shows, swap meets.
Auction Houses
Radius: 0.25 miles
Equipment auctions, estate sales. Buyers actively looking to purchase.
03 — Campaign Structure

How to Set It Up

Step 1: Create Custom Audiences

In Facebook Ads Manager, go to Audiences → Create Audience → Custom Audience. For each location, you'll create a geofence using the "Location" targeting option.

Step 2: Layer Demographics

Don't just target location. Add these filters:

  • Age: 35-65 (primary buyers)
  • Interests: Farming, Agriculture, Tractors, Rural Living, Livestock
  • Behaviors: Farmland owners, Rural residents

Step 3: Set Retargeting Window

Target users for 30 days after they visit your geofenced locations. This gives you multiple touchpoints as they research.

Pro tip: Create separate ad sets for each location category (competitors, farm stores, events). This lets you tailor messaging and track which locations perform best.

04 — Ad Creative

What to Show Them

For Competitor Locations

Lead with value and differentiation:

  • "6-Year Warranty on Every TYM Tractor"
  • "Local Service. Local Parts. Local People."
  • "Compare Before You Buy—Stop by JJ Riggs"

For Farm Supply Shoppers

Lead with seasonal relevance:

  • "Spring Prep Starts Here—TYM Tractors from $199/mo"
  • "Get More Done This Season"
  • "Built for Stevens County Winters"

For Event Attendees

Build local credibility:

  • "Your Neighbor's Favorite Dealer"
  • "25-73 HP Tractors in Stock Now"
  • Include Google review quotes
05 — Budget & Timing

Recommended Spend

Campaign Type Daily Budget Monthly Total
Competitor Geofencing $15-25/day $450-750
Farm Supply Locations $10-15/day $300-450
Events (when active) $20-30/day Varies
Total Recommended $45-70/day $1,350-2,100

Seasonal Timing

  • Peak Season (Feb-May): Increase budget 50%. Spring buying season.
  • Summer: Maintain baseline. Focus on hay/mowing equipment.
  • Fall (Sept-Nov): Increase budget 25%. Year-end buying, snow prep.
  • Winter (Dec-Jan): Reduce spend. Focus on retargeting warm leads.
06 — Metrics to Track

Measure What Matters

Focus on these KPIs to evaluate campaign performance:

  • Reach: How many unique people saw your ads Target: 5,000+/month
  • Frequency: Average times each person saw your ad Target: 3-5x
  • CTR: Click-through rate Target: 1.5%+
  • Cost per Click: What you pay for each click Target: Under $2
  • Store Visits: If enabled, track foot traffic Track trend

Attribution tip: Ask every customer who walks in: "How did you hear about us?" Track this manually to connect ad spend to sales.

07 — Quick Start Checklist

Launch in 1 Hour

  • List 5-10 competitor/farm store addresses
  • Create a new campaign in Facebook Ads Manager
  • Set objective to "Traffic" or "Store Visits"
  • Add location targeting with 0.25-0.5 mile radius
  • Layer age (35-65) and agricultural interests
  • Upload TYM to ROLL creative (or similar)
  • Set daily budget at $25-50
  • Enable "Advantage+ Placements" for broad reach
  • Schedule review after 7 days
