01 — Executive Summary

The Big Picture

JJ Riggs Equipment is the only authorized TYM tractor dealer in northeastern Washington, serving Stevens County and surrounding rural communities from their lot in Colville. The business also carries Bad Boy Mowers, Iron Craft and Braber implements, and Old Hickory Sheds.

This Facebook Ads strategy is designed to do three things:

Goal 1

Drive Lot Traffic

Get qualified buyers to visit the dealership in Colville. Every tractor is a test-drive-and-buy decision. Facebook builds the awareness and urgency; the lot closes the deal.

Goal 2

Own the Local Market

Stevens County has one TYM dealer. Every farmer, rancher, and property owner within 75 miles should know that. Geofencing competitors and blanketing the region builds unbeatable local awareness.

Goal 3

Build a Lead Pipeline

Not everyone buys today. Capture emails, phone calls, and website visits. Retarget warm audiences. When spring hits and they need iron, JJ Riggs is the first name they think of.

Core Positioning: Family-owned. Straight talk. Fair prices. We service what we sell. No corporate runaround — just a handshake, a good deal, and a tractor that works as hard as you do.

03 — Audience Architecture

Who We're Talking To

Every dollar spent on Facebook is only as good as the audience it reaches. Here's how we structure targeting across campaigns.

Core Audience

The Working Landowner

  • Age 35-65
  • Stevens County + 50-mile radius
  • Interests: farming, tractors, agriculture, livestock, rural living, land management
  • Behaviors: farmland owners, rural residents

This is the bread-and-butter buyer. They own property, they work it themselves, and they need reliable equipment at a fair price.

Expanded Audience

The Spokane Acreage Buyer

  • Age 30-55
  • Stevens County + 75-mile radius (includes Spokane metro)
  • Interests: hobby farms, homesteading, landscaping, property management
  • Behaviors: recent home buyers, small business owners

Newer to rural life. Buying their first tractor. May not know TYM yet. Price-sensitive but aspiring. The $199/mo message resonates hard here.

Geofenced Audience

The Competitor Shopper

  • Visited competitor lots (Kubota, John Deere, Case IH dealers)
  • Visited farm supply stores (Coastal, North 40, feed stores)
  • Attended ag events (county fairs, livestock shows, auctions)
  • 30-day retarget window

They're actively in the market. They've been to a competitor. Hit them with the 6-year warranty and "compare before you buy" message.

Retarget Audience

The Warm Lead

  • Website visitors (last 30-90 days)
  • Facebook/Instagram engagers
  • Video viewers (50%+ watch time)
  • Email list subscribers

They already know JJ Riggs. They clicked, watched, or visited. Now we stay in front of them until they're ready to buy.

Audience Layering: Never target a single audience in isolation. Layer demographics + interests + behaviors for precision. Example: Age 35-55 + Farming interest + Farmland owner behavior + Stevens County location = high-intent prospect.

04 — Full-Funnel Framework

Awareness to Action

Every campaign maps to a stage of the buying journey. We don't just run ads — we build a pipeline that moves people from "never heard of JJ Riggs" to "just bought a TYM."

TOP — Awareness
Brand videos, seasonal posts, "Meet the Fleet" content. Reach new people.
MID — Consideration
Carousel ads, comparison content, geofencing. Educate and differentiate.
BOTTOM — Conversion
Retargeting, inventory alerts, urgency CTAs. Drive calls and lot visits.

Service reminders, referral asks, review generation. Keep customers loyal.

06 — Creative Strategy

What the Ads Look Like

JJ Riggs doesn't talk like a corporation. The ads shouldn't either. Here's the creative framework.

Brand Voice Rules

Do

  • Talk like a neighbor, not a salesman
  • Use real numbers ($199/mo, 6-year warranty, 25+ reviews)
  • Show real equipment on real dirt
  • Keep copy short and punchy
  • Lead with the benefit, not the feature
  • Use "we" and "you" — it's a conversation

Don't

  • Use stock photos or generic imagery
  • Write paragraphs of ad copy — no one reads it
  • Trash competitors by name (let the warranty speak)
  • Use jargon the buyer doesn't care about
  • Oversell — this audience respects honesty
  • Use emojis excessively (one or two max)

09 — KPIs & Measurement

How We Know It's Working

Platform Metrics (Meta Ads Manager)

Metric Target Warning Action If Below
Click-Through Rate (CTR) 1.5%+ carousel, 2%+ geofencing Below 1% Refresh creative, test new hooks
Cost Per Click (CPC) Under $2.00 Above $3.00 Narrow audience, improve relevance
Monthly Reach 10,000+ Below 5,000 Increase budget or expand radius
Frequency 3-5x per user Above 7x Rotate creative to prevent fatigue
Video View Rate 25%+ (ThruPlay) Below 15% Shorten videos, stronger hook
Landing Page Views 300+/month Below 150 Check link, improve ad relevance

Real-World Tracking

Facebook can't track everything. These manual methods close the loop:

  • "How did you hear about us?" — Ask every single customer who calls or walks in. Track answers in a simple spreadsheet.
  • Unique landing page URL — Use jjriggsequipment.com/spring-sale (or similar) exclusively for Facebook ads to isolate traffic.
  • Call tracking number — Consider a dedicated number for Facebook ads (Google Voice works) to track inbound calls from ads.
  • Facebook Pixel — Install on all website pages to track conversions, build retarget audiences, and optimize delivery.
  • UTM parameters — Tag every ad link: ?utm_source=facebook&utm_medium=cpc&utm_campaign=[campaign-name]

Weekly Review Cadence

  • Monday: Check weekend performance. Kill any ad below 0.8% CTR after 3+ days.
  • Wednesday: Mid-week check. Adjust budgets based on pace. Reallocate from underperformers.
  • Friday: Weekly report. CTR, CPC, reach, spend. Note any creative fatigue (frequency > 6).
  • Monthly: Full review. Compare to targets. Plan next month's creative rotation. Update seasonal messaging.
10 — Launch Checklist

Get Live in 7 Days

Everything needed to go from strategy to live ads.

Day 1-2: Foundation

  • Verify Facebook Business Manager access and admin roles
  • Install Facebook Pixel on jjriggsequipment.com (all pages)
  • Set up Meta Ads Manager payment method and spending limits
  • Create Custom Audiences: website visitors (30, 60, 90 days)
  • Create Custom Audiences: Facebook page engagers (90 days)
  • Create Saved Audiences for each targeting segment (Core, Expanded, Geofenced)

Day 3-4: Creative

  • Finalize TYM to ROLL 5-slide carousel images (use real lot photos)
  • Finalize Implements carousel images (Iron Craft & Braber on the lot)
  • Create 3 Facebook feed ad variations (1200x628) from ad template library
  • Create 2 Story ad variations (1080x1920) from story templates
  • Write 3 versions of primary ad copy for A/B testing
  • Record 30-60 second video with Andrew (use landing page script as guide)

Day 5-6: Campaign Build

  • Build Campaign 1: TYM to ROLL — carousel + feed ad, Core audience
  • Build Campaign 2: Geofencing — Tier 1 competitors, Tier 2 farm stores
  • Build Campaign 3: Implements — carousel + feed ad, Expanded audience
  • Build Campaign 5: Retargeting — website visitors + page engagers
  • Set all campaigns to "Lowest Cost" bid strategy
  • Add UTM parameters to every ad link
  • Set daily budgets per campaign per the budget table above
  • Schedule Campaign 4 (Seasonal) for the current season's messaging

Day 7: Launch

  • Review all ads in Ad Preview tool before publishing
  • Double-check all links point to correct pages
  • Verify Pixel fires on landing page (use Facebook Pixel Helper extension)
  • Turn on campaigns — start with 50% budget, ramp to full over 3 days
  • Set calendar reminder: first performance review in 48 hours
  • Brief Andrew and team: "If someone says Facebook, write it down"

First 14 Days: Don't panic. Facebook's algorithm needs 3-7 days to optimize delivery. Resist the urge to change everything after Day 1. Let data accumulate, then make informed adjustments at the Week 2 mark.
