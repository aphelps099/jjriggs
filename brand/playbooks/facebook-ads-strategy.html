02 — Geofencing Conquest

Location-Based Conquest Ads

How “geofencing” actually works on Facebook: Meta doesn’t offer true device-level geofencing. What it offers is radius-based location targeting — you drop a pin on the map and set a 1–50 mile radius. Facebook then serves ads to people who live in, recently visited, or are traveling through that area.

Target Locations

Tier 1 — Competitor Dealerships (5-mile radius pins)

Other tractor/equipment dealers within 100 miles — John Deere, Kubota, Kioti dealers in Spokane, Republic, Chewelah, and Kettle Falls. Plus any mower/outdoor power equipment dealers nearby.

Tier 2 — Agricultural Hubs (3–5 mile radius pins)

Farm supply stores (Tractor Supply Co, local feed stores), agricultural co-ops in Stevens County, livestock auction yards, NE Washington Fair / Colville fairgrounds (seasonal).

Tier 3 — Broad Rural Coverage (25–40 mile radius)

Wide radius around Colville, WA. Layer with interest targeting: Farming, Tractors, Landscaping, Rural Property, Livestock.

Video Production Tips for Andrew

01
Use your phone. Authentic > polished. iPhone in landscape mode, steady hand or cheap tripod.
02
Film early morning or late afternoon for the best light on your property/lot.
03
Get b-roll first: Film 10–15 short clips of tractors working, the shop, the landscape. 5–10 seconds each.
04
Record audio separately if it’s windy — or do voiceover indoors later.
05
Captions are mandatory — 85% of Facebook video is watched on mute.
06
Use CapCut (free app) to edit on your phone with auto-captions.
07
The “stuck on hold” scene can be staged simply — someone holding a phone looking frustrated. Customers will relate instantly.

06 — Tracking & Measurement

Setup & KPIs

Setup Checklist

  • Meta Pixel installed on jjriggsequipment.com
  • Key events configured: PageView, ViewContent (tractor pages), Contact (phone click or form submit)
  • UTM parameters on all ad links: ?utm_source=facebook&utm_medium=paid&utm_campaign=[campaign-name]
  • Google Analytics 4 connected to verify traffic
  • Phone call tracking (consider CallRail or similar for ~$45/mo)

KPIs to Watch Weekly

Metric Target Range
CPM (cost per 1,000 impressions) $8–$20 (rural audiences are cheaper)
CTR (click-through rate) 1.5%+ for feed ads
CPC (cost per click) Under $2.00
Video view rate (ThruPlay) 15%+
Link clicks to website Track weekly trend
Phone calls / directions clicks Primary conversion signal

Attribution tip: Ask every customer who walks in: “How did you hear about us?” Track this manually to connect ad spend to actual sales. This is the most reliable signal for a local dealer.

07 — Launch Sequence

5-Week Rollout

Week 1 — Setup
Foundation
Install Meta Pixel. Film video b-roll. Take product photos for carousel. Set up ad account and build all campaigns in draft.
Week 2 — Launch Phase 1
Geofencing + Carousel Go Live
Launch geofencing campaign (Ad Set 1: competitors first). Launch carousel campaign. Monitor daily.
Week 3 — Launch Phase 2
Video Campaign Goes Live
Launch video campaign. All three campaigns now running. Monitor daily.
Week 4 — Optimize
Review & Scale
Review performance across all campaigns. Kill underperformers. Scale winners by 20%. Build retargeting audiences from collected data.
Week 5+ — Expand
Retargeting & Refresh
Launch retargeting campaigns. Refresh creative monthly. Test new angles. Review and adjust strategy based on performance data.
