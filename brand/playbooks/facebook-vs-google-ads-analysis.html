Ad Platform Analysis

Facebook Ads vs Google Ads
for JJ Riggs Equipment

Which platform makes more sense for a rural tractor dealership in Stevens County, WA? Here's the honest breakdown.

The Short Answer

Facebook (Meta) ads are the better primary platform for JJ Riggs right now. Google Ads has a role, but it's secondary. Here's why: nobody is Googling "JJ Riggs" yet at scale. Your job is to create demand in a 75-mile radius, not capture existing demand. That's Facebook's entire strength.

The right move: Lead with Facebook. Layer in Google strategically. Don't choose one. Sequence them correctly.

01 — What Each Platform Actually Does

Different Jobs, Different Strengths

Facebook / Meta Ads

Creates Demand

People aren't searching for tractors. They're scrolling. You interrupt them with something that stops the scroll — a TYM on dirt, a $199/mo price tag, a "your neighbor just bought one" testimonial.

  • Builds brand awareness from zero
  • Visual storytelling (equipment on real property)
  • Precise geographic + interest targeting
  • Geofencing competitor lots
  • Retargeting warm audiences
  • Cheap to test ($10-20/day to learn)
Google Ads

Captures Demand

Someone types "tractor dealer near Colville WA" or "TYM tractors for sale." They already want what you sell. Google puts you in front of them at the exact moment of intent.

  • Captures high-intent search traffic
  • Works when people already know the category
  • "Near me" and local searches
  • Google Maps / Local Service Ads
  • Higher cost-per-click in competitive keywords
  • No visual storytelling — just text
02 — Why Facebook Wins for JJ Riggs

Five Reasons Facebook Is the Lead Platform

1. You're Building Awareness, Not Capturing It

JJ Riggs is the only authorized TYM dealer in NE Washington. That's a massive advantage — but only if people know it. Most property owners within 75 miles have never heard of TYM. They don't know they can get a compact tractor for $199/mo. They're not searching for it because they don't know it exists. Facebook puts this in front of them before they ever think to Google it.

2. Equipment Is Visual and Emotional

A TYM T474 on a 10-acre plot with mountains in the background tells a story no Google text ad ever could. Carousel ads showing the full lineup. Video of iron moving dirt. Before-and-after property shots. This is scroll-stopping content that makes people want something they didn't know they needed.

3. The Audience Is on Facebook

Rural Washington, ages 35-65, property owners, farmers, ranchers, hobby homesteaders. This demographic lives on Facebook. They're in local buy/sell groups. They share posts about equipment. They scroll in the evening after a day of work. This is where you meet them.

4. Geofencing Is a Competitive Weapon

You can target people who physically visited a Kubota or John Deere dealer. When they open Facebook later, they see: "Before you overpay for a Kubota, check out TYM. Same power. Half the payment." Google can't do this. This is Facebook-only territory, and it's one of the most powerful plays in the equipment space.

5. Lower Cost to Test and Learn

Google Ads for "tractor dealer" keywords in a competitive market can run $5-15+ per click. Facebook can reach 1,000 targeted property owners for $5-10. At your budget level ($85-155/day across campaigns), Facebook gives you 5-10x more reach and data than Google would.

04 — Where Google Ads Makes Sense

Don't Ignore Google. Use It Strategically.

Google isn't wrong for JJ Riggs — it's just not the lead platform. Here's where it earns its spot:

High Value

Brand Search Capture

As Facebook builds awareness, people will Google "JJ Riggs" or "JJ Riggs Equipment Colville." A cheap brand search campaign ($5-10/day) ensures you own those clicks — not a competitor or aggregator.

High Value

Google Business Profile

Free. Already working for you (4.9 stars, 47 reviews). Keep feeding it with reviews, photos, and posts. This shows up in "tractor dealer near me" searches without paying a dime.

Medium Value

Local Service Keywords

"Tractor repair Colville" or "equipment service Stevens County" — these are urgent, high-intent searches. A small budget here captures people who need you right now.

Medium Value

TYM Brand Keywords

"TYM tractors for sale" or "TYM dealer Washington" — low volume but high intent. People searching the brand name are serious. Cheap to own these keywords since competition is low.

What to avoid on Google: Don't bid on broad keywords like "tractors" or "farm equipment." These are national-level battles with $10-20+ per click. You'll burn your budget in hours competing against John Deere and Kubota's corporate ad spend. Stay hyperlocal and brand-specific.

05 — The Right Sequence

How to Layer Both Platforms

1
Now

Lead with Facebook

Run the full Facebook strategy: TYM to ROLL, geofencing, implements, seasonal campaigns, retargeting. This is your primary engine for building awareness and driving lot traffic. Allocate 80-90% of ad budget here.

2
Layer In

Add Google Brand + Local

Set up a small Google campaign ($10-15/day) to capture brand searches and local service keywords. Optimize your Google Business Profile. This catches the people Facebook warms up.

3
Scale Later

Expand Google When Ready

Once JJ Riggs is a known name in the region and search volume grows, expand Google spend on category keywords. But only when the data justifies it — not before.

06 — Bottom Line

The Verdict for JJ Riggs

Facebook is your offense. Google is your defense.

Facebook creates demand where none exists. It turns property owners who've never heard of TYM into people who want one. It geofences your competitors. It builds a retargetable audience you own. It does all of this with visual content that resonates with your exact buyer demographic in rural NE Washington.

Google captures the demand Facebook creates. When someone sees your ad, thinks about it for a week, then Googles "JJ Riggs Colville" — Google makes sure they find you. But without Facebook running first, there's nothing to capture.

For a family-owned equipment dealer in Stevens County building regional awareness: Facebook first, Google second. Not either/or — both, in the right order.

Already built and ready to go: JJ Riggs already has a complete Facebook Ads master strategy, campaign playbooks, creative templates, geofencing plan, and landing pages built out. The infrastructure is there. The play is to execute it, measure results, and layer in Google as the brand grows.
