Where The Site Stands Today

Quick audit of what's working, what isn't, and what needs to change.

  • Bad Boy is buried — only Bad Boy Mowers is visible. No Bad Boy Tractors presence at all.
  • TYM is currently leading the navigation and homepage, which is out of sync with the new direction of the business.
  • Navigation still uses the legacy /branson-tym-tractors/ URL from the pre-TYM era — hurts SEO and credibility.
  • Homepage is visually strong but light on conversion elements — no financing CTA, no testimonials, no clear path from visitor to quote request.
  • Footer has no sitemap links, which limits internal SEO and crawlability.
  • Platform is solid — WordPress + Flatsome + WooCommerce (catalog mode) + Gravity Forms + Rank Math. We're refining, not rebuilding.

What The New Homepage Does

Organic traffic is already flowing — 400+ Google clicks in the last 30 days. The homepage redesign is built to turn that traffic into quote requests and showroom visits.

Conversion-First Design Goals

Lead with Bad Boy. New hero featuring Bad Boy Tractors as the primary brand. TYM repositioned as supporting inventory below the fold.

Clear path to action. Every section ends with a visible CTA — Get a Quote, See Inventory, Schedule Service, Apply for Financing.

Trust signals. Customer testimonials, Google review widget, and local Colville roots featured prominently — Andrew's reputation is a conversion asset we're not currently using.

Financing visible. Dedicated financing CTA on the homepage. Most tractor buyers need financing and most current visitors don't know JJ Riggs offers it.

Bad Boy-first navigation. Bad Boy Tractors becomes the primary TRACTORS entry. TYM listed as a secondary item. Footer rebuilt with full sitemap-style links.

New Bad Boy Tractor Pages — Inside The Site

The homepage brings traffic in. These interior pages are where the sale actually happens.

Dedicated Product Pages For Every Series

We build out a full set of Bad Boy Tractor pages living under /bad-boy-tractors/ — a main category hub plus individual pages for each series.

Main hub: /bad-boy-tractors/ — overview, lineup comparison, "why Bad Boy" section, clear quote CTA.

Series pages: one dedicated page per series (10 Series sub-compact, 25/30 Series compact, 40 Series, new 50 Series) — each with model specs, photos, use cases, financing callout, and quote request form.

Supporting SEO pages: "Bad Boy Tractors in Washington State," "TYM vs Bad Boy Compact Tractor Comparison," plus seasonal/use-case content — these feed the organic traffic that's already driving 400+ clicks a month.

Every interior page follows the same conversion pattern: hero → specs → use cases → financing → quote form. Consistent, fast, built to close.
